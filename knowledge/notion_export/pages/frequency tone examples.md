Here are detailed frequency/tones examples for creating binaural beats, tailored to specific purposes. Each example includes primary tones (one for each ear), the resulting beat frequency, and how you can use them effectively.

---

### 1. Deep Relaxation and Sleep (Delta Waves: 0.5–4 Hz)

- Purpose: Inducing deep sleep, a meditative state, or relief from anxiety.
- Frequencies:
  - Left Ear: 94 Hz
  - Right Ear: 96 Hz
  - Beat Frequency: 2 Hz (Delta range)
- Suggestions:
  - Blend this with soft, ambient sounds like ocean waves or a faint heartbeat.
  - Use alongside suggestions about "drifting off deeper and deeper into sleep."
---

### Tips for Combining and Customizing These Frequencies

1. Primary Tones: Keep them below 1000 Hz for comfort. Most humans perceive frequencies under 400 Hz as calming.
1. Length: Create a track lasting 20–60 minutes, depending on the activity (e.g., longer for sleep, shorter for focus).
1. Volume Balance:
  - Binaural beats: Subtle and slightly lower than ambient sounds.
  - Background music/sounds: Do not overpower the tone.
1. Experimentation:
  - Adjust base frequencies to match your personal tastes. As long as the difference fits the target brainwave range, the result is effective.
1. Overlay Layers:
  - Add subtle loops like birdsong, wind, or harmonics for richness.
