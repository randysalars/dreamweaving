- 1. Session: "Colors of Life: Vincent van Gogh’s Starry Canvases"
  Theme: Passion and Emotion

  Historical Figure: Vincent van Gogh, renowned for his intense and emotion-driven works.

  Meditation Focus: Visualize yourself under a starry night sky, each brushstroke adding depth to your feelings. This meditation encourages embracing intense emotions, reflecting van Gogh's passionate artistry.

- 2. Session: "Dream Weaver: Salvador Dali’s Surreal Vistas"
  Theme: Creativity and Imagination

  Historical Figure: Salvador Dali, a surreal artist known for his flamboyant and imaginative works.

  Meditation Focus: Picture yourself in a Dali-esque landscape, where normal rules don't apply. As you traverse this landscape, let it inspire creativity and boundless imagination.

- 3. Session: "Dance of Light: Claude Monet’s Impressionist Dawn"
  Theme: Perception and Hope

  Historical Figure: Claude Monet, a foundational figure in the Impressionist movement.

  Meditation Focus: Picture yourself by a serene pond at sunrise, the world coming to life in soft brushstrokes of light. This meditation focuses on perception, interpreting life optimistically as Monet did.

- 4. Session: "Resilient Shards: Kintsugi with Katsushika Hokusai"
  Theme: Healing and Resilience

  Historical Figure: Katsushika Hokusai, one of the most famous ukiyo-e painters and printmakers in Japan.

  Meditation Focus: Picture yourself mending a broken object with gold, appreciating the beauty of its unique fractures. Drawing parallels to Hokusai's evident respect for everyday objects and Japanese kintsugi art, this session is about healing and resilience.

- 5. Session: "River of Consciousness: Frida Kahlo’s Vivid Reflections"
  Theme: Self-Exploration and Strength

  Historical Figure: Frida Kahlo, known for her powerful self-portraits and representation of the female experience.

  Meditation Focus: Visualize painting your own self-portrait, each layer revealing deeper truths about yourself. Like Kahlo's work, this session encourages self-exploration and embracing inner strength.
