# 🧩 1. What Is a Brain State?

A brain state is a temporary configuration of electrical, chemical, and structural patterns that encode:

- Thoughts
- Emotions
- Sensory processing
- Attention
- Intentions
- Memories
- Pathologies (e.g., trauma loops, depression attractors, anxiety circuits)
Every brain state sits on a neural manifold—a geometric “shape” made of billions of neurons firing in patterns.
