Yes, there are several popular binaural beat frequencies that people commonly use, especially in meditation, focus, hypnosis, sleep, and even spiritual practices. These have become standards in many audio sessions and apps because users consistently report positive effects. Here’s a quick rundown of the most popular and widely used frequencies:

---

### Most Popular Binaural Beat Frequencies

### Delta (0.5 – 4 Hz)

- Uses: Deep sleep, unconscious meditation, profound healing, restoration.
- Popular Beats:
  - 1 Hz (very deep sleep)
  - 2 Hz (deep, regenerative relaxation)
### Theta (4 – 8 Hz)

- Uses: Meditation, creative visualization, inspiration, emotional release, deep states of relaxation, hypnosis.
- Popular Beats:
  - 4 Hz (trance, deep meditation, hypnosis)
  - 6 Hz (lucid dreaming, deep emotional healing)
  - 7.83 Hz (the Schumann resonance: “Earth’s frequency,” thought to synchronize well with human consciousness)
### Alpha (8 – 14 Hz)

- Uses: Relaxed focus, light meditation, stress reduction, “flow state.”
- Popular Beats:
  - 10 Hz (enhance alert relaxation, used for creativity and superlearning)
  - 12 Hz (relaxed but alert state, great for relaxed focus or prepping for performance)
### Beta (14 – 30 Hz)

- Uses: Active thinking, concentration, peak mental focus, analytical problem-solving.
- Popular Beats:
  - 15 Hz (focus, concentration, mental clarity)
  - 18 Hz – 20 Hz (intense focus or productivity sessions)
### Gamma (30 – 50 Hz)

- Uses: High-level information processing, insight, expanded consciousness, cognitive enhancement.
- Popular Beats:
  - 40 Hz (stimulating higher perception, intensive cognitive processing)
---

### Special Mentions

- Schumann Resonance (7.83 Hz): Many report profound grounding and peaceful sensations; extremely popular in spiritual and “Earth connection” circles.
- 528 Hz Tone (not a binaural beat, but a pure tone): Known as the “Love Frequency,” used for DNA repair/“healing” sessions—a lot of people combine this tone with an Alpha or Theta binaural beat.
---

### How People Use These

- Meditation and hypnosis tracks almost always use Delta or Theta.
- Study and productivity playlists use Alpha and low Beta.
- “Lucid dreaming” tracks frequently use Theta, often around 6 Hz or 7 Hz.
- Sleep tracks often feature a progression: starting at Alpha, slowly drifting down into Delta.
