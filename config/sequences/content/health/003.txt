Subject: The Quiet Whispers of Your Body

Hello,

I’ve been thinking about how our bodies speak to us—not in loud declarations, but in whispers. A slight tightness in the chest, a heaviness in the shoulders, or even a subtle restlessness in the legs. These aren’t alarms to be silenced, but quiet invitations to listen. Many people carry this quietly, noticing these signals but unsure if they’re worth attention. You’re not strange for feeling this, and it doesn’t mean something is wrong.

What if, just for a moment each day, you paused to hear what your body is saying? Not to fix it or push through, but simply to be with it. I’ve found that a small, intentional check-in—maybe while sipping morning tea or before bed—can reveal so much. Place a hand on your heart or belly, breathe, and ask, “What do I feel right now?” There’s no need to label it as good or bad. Just notice. This doesn’t have to be a big ritual; it can be as brief as a single breath.

I remember a time when I ignored these whispers until they became shouts—a knot in my back that I pushed past for weeks, only to find myself unable to move one morning. It wasn’t a failure, just a reminder that my body wasn’t my enemy; it was trying to communicate. You don’t have to fix this immediately, or even fully understand it. Sometimes, acknowledging the whisper is enough to shift something small, to create a little more space inside.

If this resonates, I invite you to try a daily check-in, even for a minute. Let it be messy or imperfect. There’s no right way to listen to yourself. I’m here, walking this slow path alongside you, learning as I go.

With peace,  
Randy
