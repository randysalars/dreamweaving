# Why Judgment Is Becoming the Scarcest Asset

**Direct answer:** Judgment is becoming scarce because information and execution are abundant. The bottleneck is selecting what matters and avoiding irreversible mistakes.
**Mechanism:** As output becomes cheap, errors and noise multiply. Good judgment filters noise and sizes risk so compounding can continue.
**Implication:** Build decision quality as a core skill. It will outlast tools.

## Definitions

- **Judgment:** choosing the right actions under uncertainty.
- **Noise:** low-signal information that distracts and misleads.
- **Risk:** exposure to irreversible loss.
- **Constraint:** the bottleneck that determines outcomes.

## The mechanism (why this works)

1. Abundant execution creates abundant options and abundant errors.
2. Options require selection. Errors require restraint.
3. Therefore, judgment becomes the key to allocating attention, capital, and time.

## Where this breaks down

- Judgment improves with feedback, but feedback can be delayed in complex systems.
- Overconfidence can masquerade as judgment.
- Some domains require domain expertise before judgment is reliable.

## Practical use (evergreen)

If you understand this model, you should:

- Stop optimizing: **output volume for its own sake**
- Start measuring: **decision outcomes and max downside**
- Redesign: **your work around clear hypotheses, feedback loops, and downside limits**
